Max Ehrmann<p>Desiderata<p>Go placidly amid the noise and haste,<l>and remember what peace there may be in silence.<l>As far as possible without surrender<l>be on good terms with all persons.<l>Speak your truth quietly and clearly;<l>and listen to others,<l>even the dull and the ignorant;<l>they too have their story.<p>Avoid loud and aggressive persons,<l>they are vexations to the spirit.<l>If you compare yourself with others,<l>you may become vain and bitter;<l>for always there will be greater and lesser persons than yourself.<l>Enjoy your achievements as well as your plans.<p>Keep interested in your own career, however humble;<l>it is a real possession in the changing fortunes of time.<l>Exercise caution in your business affairs;<l>for the world is full of trickery.<l>But let this not blind you to what virtue there is;<l>many persons strive for high ideals;<l>and everywhere life is full of heroism.<p>Be yourself.<l>Especially, do not feign affection.<l>Neither be cynical about love;<l>for in the face of all aridity and disenchantment<l>it is as perennial as the grass.<p>Take kindly the counsel of the years,<l>gracefully surrendering the things of youth.<l>Nurture strength of spirit to shield you in sudden misfortune.<l>But do not distress yourself with dark imaginings.<l>Many fears are born of fatigue and loneliness.<l>Beyond a wholesome discipline,<l>be gentle with yourself.<p>You are a child of the universe,<l>no less than the trees and the stars;<l>you have a right to be here.<l>And whether or not it is clear to you,<l>no doubt the universe is unfolding as it should.<p>Therefore be at peace with God,<l>whatever you conceive Him to be,<l>and whatever your labors and aspirations,<l>in the noisy confusion of life keep peace with your soul.<p>With all its sham, drudgery, and broken dreams,<l>it is still a beautiful world.<l>Be cheerful.<l>Strive to be happy.<p>Max Ehrmann, Desiderata, Copyright 1952.
